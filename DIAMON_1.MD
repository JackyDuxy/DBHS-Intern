# School Information

## History & Community

**Page title:** School Info
**Source:** https://dbhs.wvusd.org/apps/pages/index.jsp?uREC_ID=4410142&type=d&pREC_ID=2639693
**Last accessed:** 2026-06-10

Diamond Bar High School (DBHS) opened in 1982 and is located in the city of Diamond Bar, California. The southern portion of the city is served by the Walnut Valley Unified School District, while the northern portion is served by the Pomona Unified School District. DBHS is one of two comprehensive high schools in the Walnut Valley Unified School District. The district also offers a continuation high school, three middle schools and nine elementary schools.

Diamond Bar is a multi-ethnic, upper middle-class bedroom community stretching along the 57 and 60 freeways on the eastern edge of Los Angeles County. In 2021, the population of Diamond Bar was 53,857, the median income was $100,730, and in 2017, the median age was 38 years. The city is served by two different school districts, Pomona Unified on the north side and Walnut Valley Unified on the southern side. Diamond Bar is also an affluent and aging community. Since 2014, the segment of the population who ethnically identifies as white decreased from 30% to 21.7% and the Asian/Pacific Islander population has remained stable at 53.1%. Compared to the average community in the State of California, Diamond Bar has more foreign born residents (43.3%), more residents who speak a language other than English at home (58.1%), a higher home ownership rate (76.4%), more people who have graduated from high school (92.4%), and more residents with a college degree (50.7%). The city also has fewer people below the poverty line (5.9%).

DBHS serves approximately 2,600 students in grades 9-12. Our diverse student population is best described as "cosmopolitan" (both multi-ethnic and multi-cultural). A language other than English is spoken in the homes of over fifty percent of our student population.

We have a Pathways Communications Academy that is funded by a California Partnership Academies state grant. Other than this academy, we have no other state or federal mandates.

Parent support has been indispensable on our campus. Our "Magnificent Seven" group consists of the Brahma Boosters, Brahma Foundation, Council of African American Parents (CAAP), Chinese American Parent Association (CAPA), Hispanic Organization for Parents and Education (HOPE), Parents of India and Neighboring Countries (PINC) and Korean American Parent Association (KAPA). These groups provide financial and volunteer support that are essential to the assurance of a quality education.

The school's colors are purple and gold. Our mascot is a brahma bull. We are a member of the Hacienda League for football. We are a member of the Palomares League for all other athletic competitions.

Diamond Bar High School's code number (CEEB Code) is 050748. Students need this number to register for tests and to complete college applications.

Our school office hours are 7:30 AM to 4:00 PM, Monday through Friday.

This is the only official website of Diamond Bar High School. You can access this site by browsing to either http://dbhs.org/ or http://dbhs.wvusd.org/

### Vision Statement

Diamond Bar High School will be a premier school committed to the intellectual, physical, and social development of every student. Diamond Bar will be a community where every student's cultural heritage enriches the learning experience for all students.

### Mission

Diamond Bar will value the five "A's" of Academics, Access, Activities, Arts, and Athletics so that each student will experience a personalized educational program selected from a rigorous, rich and varied curriculum. Students will be challenged to discover their unique gifts and talents; staff members will support every student in realizing his or her potential. ## International Baccalaureate (IB)

**Page title:** IB Program
**Source:** https://dbhs.wvusd.org/apps/pages/index.jsp?uREC_ID=64173&type=d&pREC_ID=101810
**Last accessed:** 2026-06-10

The International Baccalaureate (IB) aims to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect.

The IB has four programs for students aged 3-19. These programs encourage students across the world to become active, compassionate and lifelong learners. They empower students to take ownership in their own learning and help them develop the knowledge, skills, and sense of purpose they need to thrive throughout their lives.

Diamond Bar High School offers the IB Diploma Program (DP). The IBDP is a challenging two-year curriculum, primarily aimed at students aged 16 to 19. It leads to a qualification widely recognized by the world's leading universities. The program prepares students for university and encourages them to: ask challenging questions; learn how to learn; develop a strong sense of their own identity and culture; and develop the ability to communicate with and understand people from other countries and cultures.

## IB Diploma Requirements

**Page title:** IB Diploma Requirements
**Source:** https://dbhs.wvusd.org/apps/pages/index.jsp?uREC_ID=64173&type=d&pREC_ID=101816
**Last accessed:** 2026-06-10

To earn an International Baccalaureate diploma, students must:

**Complete courses in six academic subjects, one from each of the IB six subject groups:**
- IB diploma candidates must select one subject from each of the six subject groups;
- a second subject from groups 3 or 4 may be substituted for a subject in group 6;
- three IB subjects must be taken at the higher level (HL), and three at the standard level (SL). A student is also permitted to take 4 HL and 2 SL courses.
- A student is only permitted to complete a maximum of two SL courses during junior year.

**Complete the core elements:**
- the extended essay, a personal research paper;
- the Theory of Knowledge, a senior seminar course designed to connect all of the disciplines;
- CAS, a student action requirement requiring extra-curricular work in creativity, activity, and service. IB students must meet 7 learning outcomes with their CAS projects.

All of the above must be done during students' junior and senior years.

**Academic standards:**
- Freshmen and sophomores: All students in DBHS IB prep must maintain a 3.0 or higher cumulative GPA in the Groups 1–5 academic courses — reviewed every semester. IB Prep students must meet prerequisites for the necessary prep and IB courses.
- Juniors: Prep sophomores wishing to continue must formally declare intent in early March of sophomore year. Upon completing a diploma candidate declaration, all prep students meeting prerequisites with a cumulative GPA of 3.0+ in IB subject groups 1–5 automatically continue into the IB Diploma Program. Any student with a GPA below 3.0 or who does not meet a prerequisite cannot continue.

**Graduation requirements not also required by the IB program (must be considered when developing a four-year plan):**
- Physical Education: 4 semesters during grades 9 and 10
- Social Science: US History (1 year), Civics (1 semester), Economics (1 semester)
- Health: 1 semester
- Applied Arts: 3 semesters of vocational or creative art, must include a course demonstrating computer literacy.
- The University of California and the California State University entrance requirements also include a one-year course in the visual or performing arts.

**IB Course Candidate:** Anyone choosing to take one or more (no limit) IB Courses and sitting for those exams. A student might choose this because completing an IB course and passing the assessments could earn college credit.

### Attendance FAQ

**How to report an absence/tardy:** Absences must be cleared within 3 days; tardies/late arrivals within 1 day. Methods:
- **Email** — include student's full name, student ID number, grade level, date(s) of absence, and reason.
- **Note** — sent with the student; include full name, ID number, grade, date(s), reason, and parent signature.
- **Call the attendance office** at the clerk extensions above.

**Voicemail:** If you reach voicemail, leave: student's full name, student ID number, date of absence/tardy/early leave, and specific reason. (To excuse a student early, refer to early-leave procedures.) Failure to provide this information may result in incorrect recording.

**Incorrectly marked absent:** Students should monitor attendance via Aeries regularly or obtain a printout from the Attendance Office. Address discrepancies as soon as possible (before the end of each month). Students may pick up a slip from the attendance office or GLC to clear an absence within 24 hours. Absences must be cleared within 3 days/72 hours or may result in truancy. Aeries Parent Portal: https://walnutvalleyusd.aeries.net/

**Extended absence / family emergency:** Contact the student's GLC and the Attendance Office as soon as possible. Students may use a short-term independent studies contract for absences of 3–14 consecutive days (including weekends and holidays), subject to GLC approval.

**Telehealth appointments:** Students must be released from school through the attendance office and may take a video/telephone appointment outside in the parking lot, then return (signing out and back in with a release slip).

**Sports / no practice:** Students are expected to be in class during scheduled class time; sport activities are considered a class. Students with an Athletics pass may leave early by showing physical ID with Athletics Pass.

**Finals week:** Students are expected to be in class during scheduled class time even after taking a final; other activities or assignments may be provided.

### Immunization Requirements
Before any child under age 18 is admitted to any pre-kindergarten facility or (TK/K-12) school in California, public or private, California law (17 CCR section 6065) requires a parent or guardian to present an immunization record. The record must: identify the student by name & date of birth; show the month, date, and year each required vaccine was received; have the type of vaccine; and include the name of the physician or agency who gave the vaccine. (Reference: California Immunization Handbook 2019, pg. 5.) Resources: www.ShotsForSchool.org

### Medication During School Hours
California Education Code Section 49423 allows the school nurse or other designated school personnel to assist students who must take medication during the school day. If a physician/dentist visit may result in prescribed medication or a dosage change, stop by the school office or download the Medication Required During School Consent.

The California Constitution mandates that public education be provided to students free of charge, unless a charge is specifically authorized by law for a particular program or activity.

This constitutional right of free access encompasses all educational activities, whether curricular or extracurricular, and regardless of whether credit is awarded for the educational activity.

The right of free access also prohibits mandated purchases of materials, supplies, equipment or uniforms associated with the activity, as well as the payment of security deposits for access, participation, materials or equipment.

A process that allows for a waiver process for an otherwise mandatory fee, charge or deposit does not render it constitutionally permissible.
